What to Be Careful About in Today's Numbers\n\nFirst and most important: engagement is a fixed placeholder value of 0.5 in all four regions. There is no paid connector yet to pull in actual social metrics like likes and shares, so the neutral default of 0.5 — used whenever real data is unavailable — has simply been substituted in. Today's data flags all four regions as \"engagement not measured\" (engagement_known=false). So while engagement contributes 7.5 points to each region's score, this does not mean actual reaction was hot — it is simply a placeholder filling the slot. Do not read engagement figures as a signal in either direction.\n\nSecond, today's change (Δ) is not an illusion of \"two days' worth of movement compressed into one.\" The prior calculation date is yesterday (2026-07-19), so today's Δ reflects a normal single-day change. That said, Europe's -12.63 is a rare, large single-day drop even by historical standards, so a single sharp decline should not be treated as an established trend without a few more days of confirmation. Indeed, Europe has been a high-volatility region over the past two weeks, swinging from 60.69 (7/9) down into the 30s.\n\nThird, buzz has now moved past placeholder status. Early on, buzz stays fixed at 0.5 until 5 baseline days accumulate; today, every region has 16 cumulative baseline days, so buzz is now measurement-based (buzz_known=true). Today's buzz figures can therefore be read as signal.\n\nFourth, regions with a thin evidence base can be swung significantly by a single article. Effective sample size (eff_n) is 3.6 for Europe, 3.7 for Northeast Asia, and 4.6 for Southeast Asia — all relatively small — while only North America is comparatively larger at 5.2. Notably, Europe recorded today's largest decline while also having the thinnest sample. In other words, Europe's -12.63 is directionally clear but rests on relatively thin evidence, so the magnitude of the drop should not be over-interpreted without further confirmation over the coming days.\n\nFifth, none of today's collected Gucci articles carried a publication date of exactly 2026-07-20 — all were dated July 16-19 (honestly logged in the collection notes as falling short of the minimum one-same-day-article threshold). This could reflect a lull in news ahead of the earnings release, or a same-day web-indexing delay. Today's score therefore reflects the \"most recent four-day window,\" and same-day follow-up confirmation is warranted.\n\nSixth, today's index reflects a source-pool deduplication fix. Two Kering financial news items had previously been double-counted across Northeast Asia, North America, and Europe; this has been corrected so that both items now count only once, in Europe. This correction lowered Northeast Asia's and North America's buzz and share-of-voice figures relative to the earlier draft, and as a result Northeast Asia's score actually flipped to a gain relative to that earlier draft.
