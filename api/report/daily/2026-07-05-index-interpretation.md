다룬다.\n\n## 6. 지금 숫자에서 조심할 것\n\n첫째, 오늘 세계 점수가 7점 넘게 움직여 자동 경보(§3.5 트리거)가 울렸지만, 확인 결과 실제 시장 이상 신호가 아니라 '착시 3종 세트' 때문으로 판정됐다. 하나는 이틀치 변화가 하루처럼 표시된 것(7월 4일 결측), 둘은 채점 공식을 v2.1로 바꾼 효과, 셋은 동북아 감성 판정이 되살아난 How to Read the Index\n\nGMAI compresses \"how attractive does Gucci look in this region right now\" into a single 0–100 score. Like a thermometer, its main use is taking the reading daily and comparing with yesterday: around 50 is ordinary, above 60 is good, below 45 is caution, below 30 is warning.\n\nThe score blends four ingredients. Sentiment: are people saying good or bad things about Gucci? Buzz: how much is Gucci talked about — the count of mentions across news, SNS and community. Engagement: per piece of content, how warm are the reactions — the intensity of reported likes/shares. Buzz and engagement are easy to confuse: buzz is the VOLUME of talk (how many mentions), engagement is the STRENGTH of a single piece (how hot the reaction to it was) — they measure different things. Share of voice: compared with rivals like Chanel and Louis Vuitton, how much of the conversation belongs to Gucci? Each ingredient is measured 0–1 and blended in fixed proportions — sentiment 45%, buzz 20%, engagement 15%, SOV 20%. Sentiment carries the most weight so that toxic buzz alone can't inflate the score.\n\nThe companion NSS (Net Sentiment Score) isolates sentiment on a -1 (all negative) to +1 (all positive) scale; zero means good and bad exactly cancel. New today, the table also shows \"effective sample\" — how many articles a region's score actually rests on. If you collect 12 items but most are old and their weight is shaved to near zero, the few fresh ones decide the score. Below 3, we flag it \"thin.\"\n\n## 3.
