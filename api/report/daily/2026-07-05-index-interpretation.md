How to Read the Index\n\nGMAI (Gucci Market Attractiveness Index) is a score from 0 to 100. Think of it like a report card or a thermometer: 50 is \"normal,\" and higher means the market finds Gucci more attractive.\n\nThere are five bands: 75+ strong appeal, 60–74 favorable, 45–59 neutral (normal), 30–44 caution, 0–29 warning.\n\nThe score blends four ingredients, in plain terms:\n\n- Sentiment: are people speaking well or badly of Gucci? The heaviest ingredient (45%).\n- Buzz: across news, social media, and community, how much is Gucci mentioned? Counts only the number of mentions (no reaction figures). Weight 20%.\n- Engagement: how strongly do people react per post (likes, comments, shares)? Computed only where figures are reported. Weight 15%.\n- Share of Voice: versus rival luxury brands, how much of the conversation does Gucci capture? Weight 20%.\n\nOne new rule: coverage Gucci itself generates — campaigns, staged events, ambassador appointments — does not count as \"public opinion\" (the activation-coverage discount). If more marketing automatically lifted the score, the index would become a brand brochure.
